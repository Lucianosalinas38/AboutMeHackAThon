Valorant: Valorant is a competitive first-person shooter game developed by Riot Games. In this game, two teams of five players each compete to plant or defuse a bomb or eliminate the other team. Each player selects a character with unique abilities to use during the game, and teamwork is key to winning. The game has quickly gained popularity in the esports community since its release in 2020.

League of Legends:League of Legends is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed by Riot Games. In this game, two teams of five players each compete to destroy the opposing team's nexus, a structure located in the enemy base. Players control characters called champions, each with their own unique abilities and playstyle. Teamwork, strategy, and quick decision-making are essential to succeed in this fast-paced game. League of Legends is one of the most popular esports games in the world, with a massive player base and a thriving competitive scene.

Osu!: Osu! is a rhythm game developed by Dean Herbert. In this game, players use a mouse, keyboard, or tablet to click, slide, and spin circles in time with music. The game features a wide variety of music genres, and players can create and share their own beatmaps for others to play. Osu! is known for its high skill ceiling and the intense physical and mental challenges it presents to players. It has a dedicated player base and a thriving competitive scene, with tournaments held regularly.
